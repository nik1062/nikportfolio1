How I Prepare for Tech Interviews as a College Student

From LeetCode to Real-World Projects

Preparing for tech interviews is not just about solving 100 LeetCode problems. It’s about understanding core concepts, practicing communication, and building confidence in problem-solving under pressure.

🧠 Data Structures & Algorithms First

I start by mastering topics like arrays, strings, linked lists, stacks, queues, trees, graphs, dynamic programming, and sorting algorithms. I use platforms like LeetCode, GeeksforGeeks, and NeetCode roadmap.

🛠️ Real Practice: Mock Interviews

Practicing mock interviews with friends or platforms like Pramp helps reduce nervousness. I simulate 45-minute interviews where I speak out loud while solving.

📚 CS Fundamentals Matter

I make sure I’m clear on DBMS, OS, Networking, and OOP — the usual theory rounds. I revise through short notes and YouTube revision videos 1–2 days before an interview.

💬 Behavioral & HR Prep

I practice common HR questions like “Tell me about yourself,” “What’s your strength/weakness?”, and “Describe a challenge you overcame.” I keep answers structured, honest, and example-based.

🎯 My Weekly Interview Routine

  • 🧩 1–2 DSA problems daily (medium-level)
  • 📘 Revise 1 CS concept every other day
  • 💻 Weekly mock interview
  • 📄 Resume & projects polish (ongoing)

Interviews test how well you can think and communicate. Being calm, clear, and consistent matters more than brute force. Just focus on improving every day.
